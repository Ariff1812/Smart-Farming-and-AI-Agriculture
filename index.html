Scenario Context:

Maximizing Oil Palm (Elaeis guineensis) Fresh Fruit Bunch (FFB) Yields in Malaysia

🌱 The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysian oil palm cultivation, precision agriculture uses AI to calculate the exact amount of Nitrogen (N) fertilizer needed. Insufficient nitrogen leads to stunted growth, while over-fertilization causes nutrient leaching, environmental toxicity, and a biological dip in yield due to vegetative overgrowth.

Data Model Baseline: Modeled based on yield curves adapted from the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) Agronomy Reference Datapages for mature palms on coastal soils.

🧮 The Calculus Model

Yield Function $f(x)$

f(x) = -0.0016x² + 0.64x + 12

Predicts Yield ($f(x)$, in Metric Tonnes per Hectare per Year) based on Nitrogen Input ($x$, in kg/hectare).

Rate of Change $f'(x)$

f'(x) = -0.0032x + 0.64

Calculates the marginal efficiency of the next kilogram of fertilizer added.
